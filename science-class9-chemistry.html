Acids, Bases and Salts

Introduction

Acids: Sour substances that turn blue litmus red (e.g., vinegar).

Bases: Bitter substances that turn red litmus blue (e.g., baking soda).

Salts: Formed from the neutralization of acids and bases (e.g., table salt).

Properties of Acids and Bases

Property Acids Bases
Taste Sour Bitter
Litmus Test Turns blue litmus red Turns red litmus blue
pH Value Less than 7 More than 7

The pH Scale

0-6: Acidic
7: Neutral
8-14: Basic

Examples: Lemon juice (pH 2), Water (pH 7), Soap (pH 9)

Real-World Applications

Acids: Used in food preservation (like vinegar in pickling).

Bases: Common in cleaning agents (like bleach).

Salts: Used for seasoning food (table salt) and in chemical industries.
